Phase 1: The Ham Radio

Focus: Learning & Validation

Like a ham radio operator searching for a clear signal, this phase focuses on validated learning. The goal is to find a repeatable customer acquisition path through rapid, low-cost experiments before committing significant resources.

Key Activities:

  • Developing MVPs
  • Conducting customer interviews
  • Running validation tests

Mindset:

"Is this a problem worth solving? Can we find anyone to pay for it?"

Execution Example (Series A to B)

+700% pipeline for Pared. Validated that targeted data improved call connect rates, then deployed Python scrapers to build ultra-targeted contact lists for sales.

Phase 2: The Local Radio Station

Focus: Execution & Scaling

With product-market fit established, the focus shifts to execution. Like a local radio station with a dedicated listener base, the goal is to build a scalable growth machine by optimizing core loops and investing in user retention.

Key Activities:

  • Identifying the primary growth loop
  • Dedicating teams to optimization
  • Investing in user retention

Mindset:

"How do we build a powerful, scalable machine to drive growth?"

Execution Example (Series B to C)

+70% pipeline for Code Climate within three weeks by launching and optimizing Google Ads for branded keywords.

Phase 3: The Broadcast Tower

Focus: Influence & Market Leadership

In a mature market, the company builds a moat through brand evangelism. Like a broadcast tower, it becomes a media entity, owning the market conversation and focusing on customer love.

Key Activities:

  • Investing in non-attributable channels
  • Building an owned media platform
  • Focusing on share of voice

Mindset:

"How do we win in a noisy market and build a brand that transcends our product?"

Execution Example (Expansion)

Led event series for Backstage in N.Y. and L.A., drawing 4,000 customers to each event.

Executive Summary: Solving the Revenue Bottleneck

The company's primary growth bottleneck was a hypothesized Signal-Metric Fit discrepancy.

The go-to-market relied on a lead-based (MQL) model, which is bad for complex, multi-stakeholder enterprise sales. This created significant friction between Marketing and Sales. Individual content downloads are poor indicators of an account's true buying intent.

This campaign shifted from a volume-based MQL model to a value-based Marketing Qualified Account (MQA) model. By focusing on account-level engagement signals across the entire buying committee, we doubled the effectiveness of the Marketing-Sales partnership and captured $1M in closed-won ARR from one of the largest banks in North America.
