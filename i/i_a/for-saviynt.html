The Strategic Directives

The market demands a unified approach to identity security. Saviynt's core strategy must be to displace point solutions and legacy IGA platforms by demonstrating the superior security posture and lower total cost of ownership (TCO) of a converged platform. The GTM strategy must be fundamentally aligned with these core value propositions.

1. Drive Platform Consolidation

Target enterprises struggling with the complexity and cost of managing multiple identity point solutions (e.g., separate IGA, PAM, AAG tools).

2. Modernize Legacy IGA

Focus on replacing outdated, on-premises IGA solutions with Saviynt's cloud-native, AI-powered platform to improve efficiency and reduce risk.

The Enterprise Prospecting Engine

A modern demand generation function must proactively identify and engage the right accounts at the right time. This requires a multi-layered approach combining technographic analysis, real-time buying signals, and strategic lookalike targeting.

The Demand Generation Toolbox

Identifying accounts is the first step. Engaging them with compelling, relevant content through the right channels is next. This toolbox is built on a "show, don't just tell" philosophy to pull prospects through the buying journey.

Video Ascendancy

For a complex product like Saviynt, video is a competitive necessity. A three-tiered strategy bridges the gap between what the product does and what prospects understand.

Always-On Webinar Engine

A programmatic webinar cadence is one of the most effective levers for generating a predictable stream of high-intent leads. The key is relevance: solve problems, don't just pitch products.

  • Problem/Solution Focused: Title webinars based on pain points.
  • Customer-Led Case Studies: Feature customer champions to build social proof.
  • "State of the Industry" Panels: Host thought-leadership discussions.
